The patterns are the reusable building blocks of the [reference architecture](../../architecture/00-trust-boundaries/). They are organised here by what they constrain, not by which layer they sit in.

**Capability constraint** — what the model can do.
- [Mediated tool surface](./mediated-tool-surface/) — the load-bearing one.

**Data shape** — what the model can see.
- [Data transformation patterns](./data-transformation/) — k-anonymity, pseudonymisation, generalisation, synthetic data.
- [Differential privacy on aggregates](./differential-privacy/) — the only mathematical answer to reconstruction at scale.

**Output shape** — what the model can return.
- [Egress filtering](./egress-filtering/) — deterministic post-processing on the LLM's response.

**Plane separation** — keeping instructions and data apart.
- [Spotlighting](./spotlighting/) — prompt-engineering technique, partial defence.
- [Dual-LLM pattern](./dual-llm/) — privileged model never sees untrusted content.
- [Ausblick: model-level defences](./model-level-defenses/) — research-grade techniques (CaMeL, StruQ, SecAlign).

**Operations** — what you do continuously, not at design time.
- [Audit log + anomaly detection](./audit-anomaly/) — load-bearing for incident response.

**Things not to do.**
- [Anti-patterns](./anti-patterns/) — the failure modes to recognise in your own designs.

Each page ends with a *Decision* block — a three-line answer to "should I use this here?" Skim those if you're choosing patterns under time pressure.
